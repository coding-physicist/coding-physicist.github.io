Research

My research bridges computational and experimental materials science, focusing on accelerating materials discovery through physics-informed machine learning and high-throughput DFT simulations. I specialize in developing inverse-design frameworks that combine graph neural networks (GNNs) and diffusion models to predict structure-property relationships and guide targeted discovery of energy materials.

My work spans convergence studies in Quantum ESPRESSO and VASP for electronic structure calculations, applying Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KANs) to thermoelectric property prediction, and building ML-accelerated workflows for heterogeneous catalysis activation barrier predictions. I aim to create automated computational pipelines that reduce the materials discovery cycle from years to weeks, with applications in thermoelectrics, photovoltaics, and sustainable energy technologies.

Looking forward, I envision developing physics-constrained generative models that can propose novel crystal structures optimized for specific functional properties â€” enabling true inverse design where we specify desired properties and the AI suggests synthesizable materials to match them.
