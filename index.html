About Me

My name is Vinayak.

I'm a Junior Research Fellow at IIT Kharagpur, working at the intersection of computational materials science, machine learning, and condensed matter physics.

My research bridges computational and experimental materials science — I develop inverse-design frameworks combining graph neural networks and diffusion models to predict structure-property relationships and guide targeted discovery of energy materials.

My work spans convergence studies in Quantum ESPRESSO and VASP, applying Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KANs) to thermoelectric property prediction, and building ML-accelerated workflows for heterogeneous catalysis. I aim to reduce the materials discovery cycle from years to weeks.

Looking forward, I envision developing physics-constrained generative models that propose novel crystal structures optimized for specific functional properties — true inverse design where we specify desired properties and the AI suggests synthesizable materials to match them.
